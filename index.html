What is a Chaos Draft?

A Magic: The Gathering Phenomenom

Chaos Draft in Magic: The Gathering began as a fan-made format where players mixed random booster packs from different sets to create unpredictable draft experiences. Its popularity grew over time, and Wizards of the Coast officially supported it at events like Magic Online and Grand Prix tournaments.

Chaos Drafting is a fun and unpredictable way to play Magic: The Gathering where you can build decks from a wildly mixed pool of cards. Instead of drafting from a single set or block, each booster pack comes from a completely different Magic set, often spanning decades of card history. This creates chaotic and surprising combinations, as cards that normally wouldn’t interact end up in the same draft. The result is a highly creative and entertaining draft experience, testing players’ deck-building skills and adaptability in unique ways. Chaos Drafts are popular for casual play and special events, offering a fresh twist on the traditional draft format.

MTG Chaos Draft – Rules Overview


1. Booster Pack Selection

  • Each player gets 3 booster packs, but each pack is from a different Magic set (ideally all from different sets and blocks).
  • Packs can span old and new sets — randomness is the point!
  • You can use:
    • Packs from your own collection
    • Random selections from a store or online bundle
    • Tools like Cube Cobra or MTG Chaos Draft generators

2. Drafting Process

  • Seat everyone in a circle.
  • Open 1 pack, pick 1 card, pass the rest to the left.
  • Continue drafting until all cards are picked.
  • Repeat for 2nd pack (pass right), and 3rd pack (pass left again).

3. Deck Construction

  • Build a 40-card minimum deck from the cards you drafted.
  • Add basic lands (provided by the organizer).
  • You may play as many copies of a card as you draft (not limited to 4 copies).

4. Gameplay

  • Play best-of-three matches against others in a mini-tournament or round-robin.
  • Follow normal Magic: The Gathering rules.
