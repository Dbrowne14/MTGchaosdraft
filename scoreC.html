In Magic: The Gathering chaos draft, score counters are a casual way to track and reward creative or thematic achievements during gameplay, adding an extra layer of fun beyond simply winning matches. These counters aren't part of official rules, but players assign them for cool plays, synergy-based combos, or fulfilling block-specific challenges (like casting a card from every color, assembling a tribal combo, or activating a planeswalker three turns in a row). Each player earns points throughout the event, and at the end, the highest scorer might win a bonus prize or bragging rights. Score counters help keep chaos drafts lighthearted and engaging, especially when playing with mismatched sets and unpredictable decks.
